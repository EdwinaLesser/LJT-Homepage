This work introduces the concept of in-context sharpness, a novel measure derived from the inner representations of large language models (LLMs). We show that in-context sharpness can be used to detect potential hallucinations in LLM generations, and we develop an alert mechanism that leverages this measure to reduce hallucinations in real-time.
