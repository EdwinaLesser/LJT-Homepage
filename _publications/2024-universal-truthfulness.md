This work identifies a universal truthfulness hyperplane in the hidden representations of large language models (LLMs). We show that this hyperplane can be used to distinguish between true and false statements generated by LLMs, and we propose a method to leverage this hyperplane to improve LLM truthfulness and reduce hallucinations.
